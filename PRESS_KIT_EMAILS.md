# Press-Kit Request Emails

Three brands in the Maison Masque catalogue publish thin marketing assets on their public product pages. These emails request direct press-kit access to get licensed, high-resolution imagery that the brand's own site doesn't expose. Tone is direct, business-appropriate, and emphasises the editorial placement Maison Masque offers in return.

Send from `hello@maisonmasque.com` (or whichever address you've configured as the business contact). Attach a one-page PDF of the Maison Masque homepage + the relevant product page for visual context. Send each email individually — don't BCC.

## Follow-up cadence

- Send all three initial emails on the same day (weekday, morning Korea time / afternoon Hong Kong time works best for Korean brand inboxes)
- **Wait 5 business days** before a polite nudge
- If no response after 2 weeks, follow up via the brand's Instagram DMs (especially CELDYQUÉ) — Korean cosmetics brands often monitor social DMs more actively than generic contact forms
- Keep a log of responses in `brand-response-log.md` (or similar) so future content updates can cite "as confirmed by [brand] press team on [date]"

## What to do if a brand doesn't respond

- **CELDYQUÉ** — fall back to the YesStyle CloudFront mirror for press-kit imagery. This is technically licensed distribution to retailers (which is what Maison Masque is), but maintain a file noting the source and the date mirrored in case CELDYQUÉ ever objects.
- **Heimish** — rely on the eheimish.com `glowbase_*` detail stack we've already mirrored. Slice the 5 images in CMS into editorial blocks.
- **SKIN1004** — lean harder on the INCI-derived "40.9% Centella" numeric hook as the storytelling anchor, and use custom editorial photography (stock imagery of Madagascar landscapes, traditional Korean cica harvesting, etc.) licensed from Unsplash/Shutterstock to fill the heritage section. This is less brand-pure but acceptable.
